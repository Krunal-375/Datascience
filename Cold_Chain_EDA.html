üìö How to Read These Charts: Visualization Interpretation Guide

üéØ Purpose: This guide helps you understand what each chart tells us about the cold chain data and how to interpret the patterns you see.

üìä Histogram Charts

  • X-axis: Range of values for the variable (e.g., project costs)
  • Y-axis: Number of projects/districts in each range
  • Shape tells us:
    • Right-skewed (tail extends right): Most values are low, few are very high
    • Left-skewed (tail extends left): Most values are high, few are very low
    • Bell-shaped: Values are normally distributed around the average
  • Business meaning: Shows how funding/projects are distributed across regions

üìà Box Plots

  • Box: Contains 50% of the data (25th to 75th percentile)
  • Line in box: Median (middle value)
  • Whiskers: Extend to reasonable data range
  • Dots beyond whiskers: Outliers (unusual values that need attention)
  • Business meaning: Identifies states/regions with unusually high or low funding

üîó Correlation Heatmaps

  • Color intensity: Strength of relationship between variables
  • Red/Dark colors: Strong positive correlation (both increase together)
  • Blue/Light colors: Strong negative correlation (one increases, other decreases)
  • White/Neutral: No meaningful relationship
  • Values range: -1 (perfect negative) to +1 (perfect positive)
  • Business meaning: Shows which factors influence each other

üéØ Significance Thresholds

  • Correlation > 0.7: Strong relationship worth investigating
  • Correlation 0.3-0.7: Moderate relationship
  • Correlation < 0.3: Weak relationship
  • P-value < 0.05: Statistically significant result

üí° What to Look For

  1. Outliers: Districts/states that are very different from others
  2. Patterns: Consistent trends across regions or time
  3. Gaps: Areas with unusually low funding or activity
  4. Concentrations: Where most funding/projects are focused

üìö Bivariate Analysis: How to Read Relationship Charts

üéØ Purpose: Understanding how two variables relate to each other and what this means for cold chain policy.

üîó Correlation Analysis

  • Correlation Coefficient (r): Measures strength of linear relationship
    • r > 0.7: Strong positive relationship (both increase together)
    • r = 0.3 to 0.7: Moderate relationship
    • r < 0.3: Weak relationship
    • r < 0: Negative relationship (one increases, other decreases)
  • P-value: Statistical significance (< 0.05 means relationship is real, not random)

üìä Scatter Plots

  • Each dot: Represents one project/district
  • Upward trend: Positive correlation (both variables increase together)
  • Downward trend: Negative correlation (one increases, other decreases)
  • Scattered dots: No clear relationship
  • Tight cluster: Strong relationship
  • Wide spread: Weak relationship

üìà Cross-tabulation Tables

  • Rows & Columns: Categories of each variable
  • Numbers in cells: Count of projects/districts in each combination
  • Percentages: Show proportional distribution
  • High numbers: Common combinations
  • Low numbers: Rare combinations

üéØ Business Interpretation

  • Strong correlations: Suggest policy levers (change one to influence the other)
  • Weak correlations: Variables operate independently
  • Outliers in scatter plots: Special cases needing investigation
  • Empty cells in cross-tabs: Market gaps or policy restrictions
